1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture used in modern computing.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 32-bit 386 processor, revolutionizing multitasking on PCs.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel's highest greenhouse gas emissions recorded; followed by major investments in energy efficiency and sustainability.

2020

RISE Strategy

Launch of Intel's RISE strategy and 2030 sustainability goals for climate, water, and waste.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Intel pledges to reach net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040 across its global operations.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage globally, significantly reducing its carbon footprint.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, driving collaborative innovation in green manufacturing.
